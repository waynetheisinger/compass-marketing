## 1. Forecast Methodology

### 1.1 How the Projected Monthly Turnover Was Constructed

The 2026 turnover forecast is built on a **plan-for-the-worst, hope-for-the-best** methodology. It is deliberately conservative — its purpose is to set a defensible baseline from which to measure the uplift that marketing activity delivers, not to promise aspirational figures with no statistical basis.

**The model works as follows:**

1. **January 2026 actual turnover is the anchor.** January's gross turnover of £54,324 is a real, achieved number — not a projection.

2. **Month-on-month seasonal shift indices are derived from 2025 historical trading data.** Each month's relationship to the previous month in 2025 is calculated as a ratio (e.g. if February 2025 was 1.394× January 2025, that multiplier is applied to January 2026 to produce February 2026's forecast).

3. **Each subsequent month is calculated by multiplying the prior month's forecast by its seasonal shift index:**

| Month | Seasonal shift (vs. prior month) | Forecast gross turnover |
|---|---|---|
| **Jan** | — (actual) | £54,324 |
| **Feb** | ×1.394 | £75,727 |
| **Mar** | ×5.753 | £435,635 |
| **Apr** | ×1.727 | £752,255 |
| **May** | ×0.969 | £728,559 |
| **Jun** | ×0.625 | £455,422 |
| **Jul** | ×0.747 | £339,973 |
| **Aug** | ×0.919 | £312,265 |
| **Sep** | ×0.703 | £219,397 |
| **Oct** | ×1.211 | £265,778 |
| **Nov** | ×1.145 | £304,236 |
| **Dec** | ×0.419 | £127,505 |
| **Total** | | **£4,071,076** |

### 1.2 Why This Approach, Not Aspirational Targets

Forecasting million-pound monthly turnovers without a statistical basis is not forecasting — it is guesswork. This model is grounded in:

- **Real data:** January actuals as the starting point
- **Proven seasonal patterns:** The exact seasonal curve the business followed in 2025
- **No marketing uplift baked in:** This is critical — the forecast assumes zero incremental impact from the marketing plan. Any uplift from the campaigns, channels, and tactics outlined in this document is therefore additive and measurable

This means:
- If April delivers £900K instead of £752K, we can attribute the £148K uplift to specific marketing activities
- If March underperforms due to weather (a known variable — the 2025 data shows March can swing significantly with late cold spells), the model already reflects that historical volatility
- We do not want to over-promise — it is far better to set a credible baseline and exceed it than to set fantasy targets and explain shortfalls

### 1.3 What This Forecast Does Not Include

- **Marketing-driven uplift:** As stated above, the forecast is the baseline with no marketing multiplier applied. The entire purpose of this marketing plan is to beat these numbers. Section 3 (Hero Product & SKU-Level Promotion Plan) details exactly how we intend to do that, product by product, channel by channel.
- **Out of Scope:** Channels over which I, Wayne Theisinger, have no control and cannot therefore make any useful impact. Therefore This plan does not include turnover from https://compassgm.co.uk/ or from the Showroom.

## 3. Hero Product & SKU-Level Promotion Plan

This section directly addresses the question: *"Where is the imagination and planning of which SKUs and when will be promoted?"*

The answer is that we do not market "everything to everyone." Each month has designated **hero products** — specific SKUs chosen for maximum margin, seasonal demand, and competitive positioning — that receive concentrated spend across all channels. Supporting SKUs ride alongside in catalogue-wide campaigns (Shopping, DPA) but the creative energy, influencer partnerships, content, and promotional budget are focused on the heroes.

### 3.1 Hero Product Selection Criteria

Every hero product must pass these filters:

| Filter | Why |
|---|---|
| **Margin** | Minimum 35% gross margin — must be able to absorb ad spend and still deliver profit. Spectrum own-label products are prioritised (highest margin in the range) |
| **Stock depth** | Minimum 50+ units in stock — no point driving demand for products that will sell out in 48 hours |
| **Competitive price position** | Must be price-competitive or offer a clear value advantage (warranty, bundle, spec) vs. top 3 competitors for that product type |
| **Seasonal relevance** | Must match the month's buying intent — nobody is buying a ride-on mower in December |
| **Content-readiness** | Must have (or we must create) video, lifestyle photography, and comparison content ready before the push begins |

#### Feed Optimisation (Foundation)

Before any ad spend, the Google Merchant Centre product feed must be airtight:

| Feed element | Action |
|---|---|
| **Titles** | Front-load with search terms: `[Brand] [Product Type] [Power Source] [Key Feature] [Model]` — e.g. "Spectrum 46cm Self-Propelled Petrol Lawn Mower SP46" |
| **Descriptions** | 150–500 words; include long-tail keywords; mention warranty and delivery |
| **GTINs/MPNs** | Ensure all SKUs have valid identifiers — critical for Shopping eligibility |
| **Product type** | Use Google's taxonomy: `Home & Garden > Lawn & Garden > Outdoor Power Equipment > Lawn Mowers` |
| **Images** | White-background hero image + lifestyle shots; 800×800px minimum |
| **Price** | Ensure sale prices are submitted with `sale_price` and `sale_price_effective_date` attributes |
| **Custom labels** | Tag by margin tier (high/medium/low), season, and stock status for campaign segmentation |

#### Campaign Architecture

```
Account
├── Performance Max — High-Margin Products (Spectrum own-label)
│   ├── Asset Group: Cordless Mowers
```

## 13. Budget Framework & Marketing Investment Case

### 13.1 Is 10% the Right Number?

The 10% of gross turnover allocation is a starting position, not a fixed rule. Here is the context:

| Benchmark | Typical range | Source |
|---|---|---|
| UK e-commerce average | 7–12% of revenue | Gartner CMO Spend Survey |
| Online-only retail (no physical stores) | 10–15% of revenue | Common industry guidance — online retailers must invest more as they have no footfall |
| Growth-stage e-commerce (scaling aggressively) | 12–20% of revenue | Typical for businesses aiming for >30% YoY growth |
| Established e-commerce (steady state) | 5–8% of revenue | Mature businesses with strong organic/repeat customer base |

**Our position:** MowDirect is an online-only retailer in a growth phase, diversifying into new channels (marketplaces, affiliates, social) for the first time. 10% is at the **conservative end** of where we should be. If the hero product strategy delivers measurable ROAS above target, the case for increasing to 12–15% becomes strong — because every additional pound spent at a positive ROAS is profit, not cost.

**The key question is not "is 10% right?" but "is the return on that 10% justifiable?"** — which is why every channel in this plan has defined KPIs, and the hero product approach allows us to measure return at the SKU level, not just the blended level.

## 14. Budget Governance & Daily Spend Management

This section addresses the question: *"How will you manage the spend day to day and ensure you do not get to month end with an overspend?"*

### 14.1 The Core Principle: Budget Is Tied to Turnover, Not Calendar

Because the marketing budget is 10% of turnover, the budget for each month is proportional to that month's revenue. This means:

- **If turnover tracks below forecast:** marketing spend must be reduced proportionally — we do not spend April's £75K marketing budget if April's turnover is tracking at 60% of forecast
- **If turnover tracks above forecast:** we can release additional budget (still capped at 10%) because the revenue is there to support it
- **The budget is a ceiling, not an entitlement** — spend is earned by revenue performance

### 14.2 Daily & Weekly Spend Controls

| Control | How it works | Who owns it |
|---|---|---|
| **Daily budget caps in ad platforms** | Google Ads, Meta, TikTok, and Pinterest all support daily budget caps. Set each platform's daily budget to the monthly allocation ÷ days in month. Platforms will not exceed the daily cap. | Marketing Manager |
| **Weekly pacing check** | Every Monday: compare actual week spend vs. planned week spend across all channels. If spend is >10% ahead of pace, reduce daily caps immediately. If behind pace, investigate underdelivery. | Marketing Manager |
| **Shopify revenue dashboard** | Real-time revenue tracking — if week 1 revenue is tracking below 25% of monthly forecast, flag immediately and consider pausing discretionary spend (TikTok, Pinterest) until revenue recovers | Marketing Manager |
| **10% rule-of-thumb checkpoint** | At any point in the month: total marketing spend to date should not exceed 10% of total revenue to date. If it does, pause non-essential channels until revenue catches up | Marketing Manager |

### 14.3 Monthly Budget Governance Process

```
Week 1 (1st–7th):
├── Review prior month's final spend vs. budget — document any variance
├── Set current month's daily budget caps across all platforms
├── Confirm hero product creative and listings are live
└── Monday pacing check #1

Week 2 (8th–14th):
├── Monday pacing check #2 — compare spend to revenue pace
├── Mid-month revenue forecast: is turnover tracking to plan?
├── If revenue is <80% of pace → reduce discretionary spend (TikTok, Pinterest, affiliates)
└── If revenue is >110% of pace → consider releasing 5% additional budget to winning channels

Week 3 (15th–21st):
├── Monday pacing check #3
├── Three-quarter-month review with full P&L snapshot
└── Adjust remaining week budgets to land on target

Week 4 (22nd–end):
├── Monday pacing check #4
├── Final week: tighten daily caps to prevent overshoot
├── Prepare month-end report: actual spend, actual revenue, actual ROAS by channel
└── Flag any channel that underperformed ROAS target — recommend pause or restructure
```

### 14.4 Overspend Prevention Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Hard cap:** No channel can exceed its monthly allocation by more than 5% without written approval | Prevents runaway spend on any single channel |
| **Soft cap:** If total spend reaches 90% of monthly budget before the 25th, all discretionary channels are paused for the remainder of the month | Protects against late-month overshoot |
| **Kill switch:** If blended ROAS drops below 3:1 for any 7-day rolling period, pause all non-brand paid spend immediately and investigate | Protects against sustained unprofitable spend |
| **Monthly reconciliation:** Actual spend vs. budget is reported within 3 working days of month end, shared with investors | Transparency and accountability |

### 14.5 Reporting to Investors

| Report | Frequency | Content |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly snapshot** | Every Monday (emailed) | Revenue vs. forecast, spend vs. budget, ROAS by channel, hero product performance |
| **Monthly deep-dive** | First week of following month | Full P&L, channel-level breakdown, hero product results, variance analysis, next month's plan |
| **Quarterly board pack** | End of quarter | Strategic review, competitive intelligence, budget reforecast, recommendations for budget adjustment |
